01 - The Art of Prioritization

The ROI of Effort Framework

Not All Tasks Deserve Equal Effort

A 2019 study in the Journal of Experimental Psychology found that people who distinguish between high- and low-stakes tasks report 31% lower stress and higher academic performance.

High-Impact Work

  • Coursework tied to your specialization
  • Genuine recruiter and professional relationships
  • Projects with real deliverables
  • Strategic rest and recovery

Busy Work

  • Perfecting formatting no one reads
  • Attending every optional event
  • Re-reading notes you already know
  • Refreshing email every 10 minutes

The Eisenhower Matrix

DO NOW

Urgent and Important

Exam tomorrow - Deadline today - Health issue

SCHEDULE

Not Urgent and Important

Career goals - Relationships - Self-care - Planning

DELEGATE

Urgent and Not Important

Some emails - Routine admin - Certain meetings

ELIMINATE

Not Urgent and Not Important

Social media spirals - Unnecessary perfecting

The Grad School Trap: Most burnout comes from spending time in the Delegate and Eliminate boxes, not from actually being too busy.

The Good Enough Permission Slip

Research by Dr. Barry Schwartz shows that “satisficers” – people who choose good enough – report higher life satisfaction than “maximizers” who over-optimize.

Graduate School Translation

Quality Result
90% paper you submit Gets graded
100% paper never turned in F
Weekly networking emails Career connections
Perfect emails never sent Silence

Time Blocking - The Career Hustle Fix

Why Recruiting Takes Over

  • Job search is infinite – there is always one more application, company, or connection
  • Without structure, recruiting anxiety bleeds into every moment
  • Cal Newport in Deep Work – The pull to always be doing something kills deep focus

The Fix

Reserve specific hours for job search. Outside those hours – it does not exist.

Done for today is not giving up. It is protecting tomorrow’s energy.

Sample Weekly Block

Day Block Task
Mon 7-8pm Applications
Wed 12-1pm Networking
Fri 6-7pm Follow-up
Weekend Off Full rest

About 3 hrs/week of focused effort vs. 20hrs of anxious scrolling

School Brain vs. Career Brain

School Brain

When – Class, studying, group projects

Mindset – Learner, analyst, collaborator

Tools – Canvas, notes, textbooks

Off trigger – Close the career portal when you open your textbook

Career Brain

When – Your designated recruiting blocks

Mindset – Marketer, relationship-builder

Tools – LinkedIn, resume, email

Off trigger – One tab rule – close Handshake when you open Canvas

Neuroscience: Context switching costs about 23 minutes of refocus time (Gloria Mark, UC Irvine). Protecting brain modes is not just strategy – it is science.

Divide by Energy, Not Just Skill

Teams that align tasks with members energy patterns outperform skill-matched teams by up to 20% on complex projects (Journal of Applied Psychology, 2014)

Old Way – Divide by Skill

  • “You are good at Excel – you do the model”
  • “You speak well – you present”
  • Results in resentment and hidden imbalance

Better Way – Divide by Energy

  • Who feels energized doing deep analysis?
  • Who works best in the morning vs. evening?
  • Who actually enjoys writing vs. visuals?

Try this: Start every group project with 10 minutes of everyone completing: I do my best work when… – it changes the dynamic completely.

Avoiding the Team Carry Trap

Why You Carry the Team

  • Fear of bad grades you cannot control
  • Conflict avoidance
  • Perfectionism
  • I am always the reliable one

Why You Should Stop

  • Breeds resentment in you
  • Robs teammates of learning
  • Accelerates your burnout fast
  • Trains them to keep relying on you

The Redistribution Script

“I want to make sure we all own a piece of this – can we each commit to X by Thursday?”

Warning Signs

  • You know everyone’s tasks better than your own
  • You are the only one checking the group chat
  • You have done it yourself more than twice
  • You feel anxious when others handle their parts
  • You rewrite others work silently

04 - Normalizing Unproductive Rest

Your Brain Needs Downtime to Perform

Rest is Maintenance - The Science

What Research Actually Shows

  • The brain’s default mode network activates during rest – this is where creativity, insight, and memory consolidation happen (Buckner et al., 2008)

  • Students who take regular breaks retain 30% more than those who study continuously (Kornell and Bjork, 2008)

  • Sleep deprivation reduces cognitive performance to the equivalent of being legally drunk (Williamson and Feyer, 2000)

  • A 10-minute walk doubles creative output in the subsequent task (Stanford, 2014)

Types of Intentional Rest

Micro-rest 5-min break every 50 min (Pomodoro)

Active rest Walk, stretch, cook (not scrolling)

Social rest Time with people who energize you

Sleep 7-9 hours. Non-negotiable.

Mind-wandering Let your brain process in the background

Intentional Rest vs. Avoidance

Intentional Rest

  • Chosen deliberately
  • Has a defined start and end
  • Leaves you feeling recharged
  • Examples – scheduled walk, dinner with friends, reading fiction, nap with alarm

Rest is giving yourself permission to stop – so you can go again.

Avoidance (Rest Disguise)

  • You fell into it to escape
  • Feels slightly guilty the whole time
  • Leaves you more anxious than before
  • Examples – 3-hour TikTok spiral, Netflix when you should be working

Avoidance does not restore – it just delays.

The tell: After intentional rest you feel ready. After avoidance you feel worse. Use this as your compass.

05 - Recognizing Red Flags Early

Burnout Does Not Arrive Overnight

The Burnout Warning System

Emotional

  • Constant irritability
  • Dread before class or work
  • Emotional numbness
  • Cynicism about your program
  • Feeling like nothing matters

Physical

  • Chronic fatigue even after sleep
  • Frequent headaches
  • Getting sick more often
  • Insomnia or oversleeping
  • Appetite changes

Academic

  • Falling behind with no catch-up plan
  • Procrastination becoming the norm
  • Confusion on topics you once knew
  • Missing deadlines for the first time
  • Avoiding professors and advisors

Based on Maslach Burnout Inventory dimensions: Emotional Exhaustion, De-personalization, Reduced Personal Accomplishment

The Traffic Light Check-In

Every Sunday, Rate Yourself

Green – Capable, rested, engaged. Keep going.

Yellow – Something is off. Managing, but barely.

Red – In distress. Need to act today.

If You Are Yellow or Red

  1. Name what is specifically overwhelming you
  2. Cancel one non-essential commitment
  3. Talk to one person (friend, mentor, advisor)
  4. Access Baruch Counseling Center – it is free

Baruch Resources

  • Counseling Center

  • Graduate Career Management Center (GCMC)

  • Student Affairs

  • Baruch Learning Community – Academic support and tutoring (SACC)

  • Graduate Advisor

They are on your side – reach out! You do not have to be in crisis to ask for help.

The 5-Minute Weekly Reset

A Simple Sunday Ritual

Answer these 5 questions every week

  1. What was my biggest win this week?
  2. What drained me the most?
  3. What is the ONE most important thing next week?
  4. Am I taking care of my body? (sleep, food, movement)
  5. Do I owe anyone a conversation?

Then write your top 3 priorities for the week on a sticky note. Put it where you will see it.
